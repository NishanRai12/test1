EDUCATION

From 1889 to 1893, Kafka attended the Deutsche Knabenschule German boys' elementary school at the Masný trh/Fleischmarkt (meat market), now known as Masná Street. His Jewish education ended with his bar mitzvah celebration at the age of 13. Kafka never enjoyed attending the synagogue and went with his father only on four high holidays a year.An ornate four-storey palatial building Kinský Palace where Kafka attended gymnasium and his father owned a shop After leaving elementary school in 1893, Kafka was admitted to the rigorous classics-oriented state gymnasium, Altstädter Deutsches Gymnasium, an academic secondary school at Old Town Square, within the Kinský Palace. German was the language of instruction, but Kafka also spoke and wrote in Czech. He studied the latter at the gymnasium for eight years, achieving good grades.[28] Although Kafka received compliments for his Czech, he never considered himself fluent in the language, though he spoke German with a Czech accent.He completed his Matura exams in 1901.

THE METAMORPHOSIS:PLOT AND SUMMARY

The metamorphosis is one of the greatest work of fiction of the 20th century. The tragicomic stories act as a form of mythology for the modern industrial age employing logic to explore the relationship between systems of power and the individual that caught up in them. The Kafka most famous stories “METAMORPHOSIS”.

Gregor Samsa awaken one morning to find himself transformed or metamorphosed into a vicious giant vermin and his greatest worry is that he gets to work on time.The story has central existentialist theme of the story is Work, capitalism, drudgery, angst and alienation mostly takes place in a single confined room. The opening lines of the book is one of the greatest literature in the world “ AS GREGOR SAMSA AWOKE ONE MORNING FROM UNEASY DREAMS HE FOUND HIMSELF TRANSFORMED IN HIS BED INTO A MONSTROUS VERMIN” The cause of Gregor transformation isn’t clearly explained .

THE METAMORPHOSIS:SYMBOLS

There is a big detachement between the human mind and the metamorphosed body of Gregor’s. He tries to reconcile his human emotions and with physical urges of this body. This details still shows that he still feels connected to his past self and he considered himself as a part of the family. One of the central theme that dominates every part of the story is if Gregor is still human and if so to what extent. Towards the end his sister starts to think of him as a lowly insect who is just a chore and inconvenience. Only the mother calls him as an unfortunate son and despite the appearance of his she still think of him as an old version of him. He can hear his sister playing violin and he is entranced by the violin. Was he an animal if music could captivate him so? The book made a clear impression that why do people continue to love the loved ones even after their death but not when they are metamorphosed into a vermin. Kafka has written this story in such a way that reader is not terrified by thought of turning into an insect but instead to be treated by the way how Gregor’s parents inhumanly treated him as if he is nothing more than just a burden The lingering thought that often is that he is afraid of dying because he hasn’t yet lived his life to the fullest and same goes for us we are the gregor samsa of our own universe who lost their hope and who lost their identity who felt like an insect among own family disgusting and misunderstood .

THE METAMORPHOSIS:CHARACTERS

GREGOR SAMSA

Gregor is the main character of the story. He works as a traveling salesman in order to provide money for his sister and parents. He wakes up one morning finding himself transformed into an insect. After the metamorphosis, Gregor becomes unable to work and is confined to his room for most of the remainder of the story. This prompts his family to begin working once again. Gregor is depicted as isolated from society and often both misunderstands the true intentions of others and is misunderstood.

GRETE SAMSA

Grete is Gregor's younger sister, and she becomes his caretaker after his metamorphosis. They initially have a close relationship, but this quickly fades. At first, she volunteers to feed him and clean his room, but she grows increasingly impatient with the burden and begins to leave his room in disarray out of spite

MR SAMSA

Mr Samsa is Gregor's father. After the metamorphosis, he is forced to return to work in order to support the family financially. His attitude towards his son is harsh. He regards the transformed Gregor with disgust and possibly even fear and attacks Gregor on several occasions. Even when Gregor was human, Mr Samsa regarded him mostly as a source of income for the family

MRS SAMSA

Mrs Samsa is Gregor's mother. She is portrayed as a submissive wife. She suffers from asthma, which is a constant source of concern for Gregor. She is initially shocked at Gregor's transformation, but she still wants to enter his room.

THE CHAR WOMAN

The charwoman is an old widowed lady who is employed by the Samsa family after their previous maid begs to be dismissed on account of the fright she experiences owing to Gregor's new form. She is paid to take care of their household duties.
